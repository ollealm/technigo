Lou's Cookies


Lou Montulli in the '90s

With an EU-law requiring all sites to inform visitors about cookies most people have at least a basic understanding how they are used to track site activity, and the most important usage is probably to know whether a user is logged in or not. A bigger privacy concern is tracking cookies, especially from third-party, that can be used to compile long-term records of individuals browsing histories.

The term cookie was coined by computer programmer Lou Montulli when working on Mosaic Netscape in 1994. His idea was to use a funktion from Unix called ”magic cookie” to store information in the browser checking whether visitors to the Netscape website had already visited the site.

Lou Montulli is also the creator of the infamous <blink>Tag</blink>, an integral part of the early internet, but now mostly unsupported. It was made as an easter-egg and he has later stated it was the worst thing that he did for the internet.
